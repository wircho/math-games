How To Play

Start at (0, 0) and set a target pair of numbers (for example, (3, 3)). Players take turns:

  • Student's turn: Add or subtract 1 to either number (keeping both numbers nonnegative)
  • Teacher's turn: Divide both numbers by any common divisor (except by 1), or pass if no such divisor exists

The student succeeds when they reach the target pair exactly.

What To Expect

The student will quickly learn that certain pairs are "safe" while others allow the teacher to divide. Landing on safe pairs lets the student make steady progress.

Helpful tip: The student can make a table and cross off pairs they want to avoid (see the cheat sheet in The Math Behind It section below for an example).

Note that (10, 10) is particularly difficult and the student can take some time to figure out.

Hint for reaching (10, 10)

You will first need to get to (1, 11).

The Math Behind It

This game teaches coprime numbers (also called "relatively prime"). Two numbers are coprime if they share no common divisors except 1.

The "snakes and ladders" aspect comes from the ups and downs of play: adding to the numbers moves you toward the goal, while landing on non-coprime pairs brings you back down.

Through repeated play, students discover which pairs are coprime by observing which ones cannot be divided. This hands-on approach helps build intuition about common divisors.
