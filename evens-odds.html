Evens & Odds (Snakes & Ladders Style Game)

← Back to games

How To Play

Set a starting number (for example, 5) and a target number (for example, 100). Players take turns:

  • Student's turn: Add any number from 1 to 9
  • Teacher's turn: Divide by 2 if the number is even, or pass if it's odd

The student succeeds when they reach or surpass the target number.

Interactive Version

While this interactive version lets you practice the game, these games are most valuable when played with a teacher or parent. The guidance, encouragement, and mathematical discussion that happens during play cannot be replaced by a screen. Teachers and parents are encouraged to try the interactive version as a way to familiarize themselves with the game before playing it with students.

What To Expect

The student might initially add 9 whenever possible to reach the target quickly. However, landing on even numbers allows the teacher to divide and bring the total back down. Through play, students begin to strategize about which numbers to add and when, learning to balance speed with avoiding even numbers.

Helpful tip: The student can write down numbers and cross off the ones they want to avoid landing on (the even numbers—see the cheat sheet in The Math Behind It section below for an example). This visual aid helps them plan their moves more carefully.

The Math Behind It

This game teaches even and odd numbers, along with division and strategic thinking.

The "snakes and ladders" aspect comes from the ups and downs of play: adding numbers moves you toward the goal, while landing on even numbers that get divided brings you back down.

Through repeated play, students discover that staying on odd numbers prevents the teacher from dividing, allowing steady progress toward the target.

Variations

Try these variations to explore other mathematical operations:

  • Divide by 3: Teacher divides by 3 when the number is divisible by 3, otherwise passes
  • Divide by 5: Teacher divides by 5 when the number is divisible by 5, otherwise passes
  • Multiple divisors: Teacher can divide by 2, 3, or 5 (whichever works), choosing strategically
  • Different ranges: Student can add numbers from 1 to 6, or 1 to 12
  • Prime Numbers (Snakes & Ladders Style Game): Teacher can divide by any divisor (except 1 or the number itself)
